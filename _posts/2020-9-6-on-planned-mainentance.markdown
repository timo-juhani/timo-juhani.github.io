Some days ago, I noticed emerging news items covering major maintenance windows of Finnish banks [OP and Nordea](https://yle.fi/uutiset/3-11524828) reported by YLE (Finnish Broadcasting Company). Because of Corona pandemia this leaves consumers in a tricky situation since cash payments are not accepted broadly at the moment, according to the article. Hence YLE reported the planned maintenance in the news.

A couple of vantage points on this:

*   Consumers and businesses should be always prepared for situations where electronic banking is not available. This means planning that looks beyond tomorrow. Nothing more to say about this.
*   Maintenance work should not be a surprise to consumers. Instead, it should be a sign of operational quality and it should be expected. The lack of it is an alarming sign. Even keeping the system "as-is" means periodic maintenance. Without maintenance any system will start to show signs of degradation within 6-12 months, based on personal experience. You should not trust systems that are not maintained continually. Especially on financial matters.
*   Since companies have largely moved to work from home policies globally the attack surface is larger than ever. In fact, it has grown exponentially in few months. We're waiting to see that exploited. That's why keeping systems safe is extremely important right now.
*   From operational perspective many maintenance activities can be automated. Automation here helps to reduce the length and the cost of the maintenance window. Therefore decreasing the impact on the consumer. However, automated workflows haven't been implemented often and many orgs are forced to labor through complex changes relying on manual work.

That's it for now. Make sure services and systems you use are maintained often. Sometimes that means accepting assets not being available. This shouldn't be a problem unless you have neglected contingency planning.

Finally, remember that the good guys are doing thankless jobs while you're sleeping and kick off their normal duty in the morning breaking their normal sleep cycle. They do this to protect you and your business.
