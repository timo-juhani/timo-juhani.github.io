"Silence means security" was a well-known statement during WW2. Today, we all carry tracking devices on our hips voluntarily and the devices are, by design, not silent at all. They expose our routines, consumption patterns, social networks and our location. To sum it up, they are a disaster from [OPSEC](https://www.tripwire.com/state-of-security/security-data-protection/opsec-everyone-not-just-people-something-hide/) perspective.

We have given our privacy in exchange to services that are allegedly free to use. Luckily many of us have realized that the cost of this "free" ecosystem thinking is turning the consumers to a product by tracking us. So, if you are traveling a lot on business and leisure, you may want to increase your professional and personal security by having a burner phone. Why? Increasingly often on-line tracking of executives and tourists have been used to complement traditional crimes like kidnapping and extortion in non- or semi-permissive environments.

A burner phone is a (if need be) disposable phone that provides anonymity. The concept is pretty simple but we all know how much private info a smartphone needs us to fill in before we are able to send a single message. Therefore choosing good tactics to build burner phones is a must. I have gathered some key considerations in this post that should help you on your path to reclaim your personal security and privacy.

*   The phone you choose as a burner should be a factory reset, used one. However, it should still receive software upgrades which is a foundational requirement for any hardened architecture.

*   Consider carefully which email address you use to register the phone's user account. In other words, using anonymous email address is required. There are well-known tactics to accomplish this but are outside the scope of this post.

*   Next in line is procuring a pre-paid SIM that you can use to register with the app store. Note: even though SIM provides a mobile network connection it is not your primary data link.

*   Choose your comms tools and keep the application landscape to the minimum. Most security professionals recommend using [Signal](https://signal.org/).

*   Choose a VPN solution that allows you to connect securely over WiFi networks.

These four points are the baseline considerations you should plan with your security team when building burner phones. That said, the post is intentionally short and omits some of the detailed configuration and procurement tactics that must be executed correctly for the burner to provide protection as intended. These tactics are often customized to meet with the expectation. Therefore, get your experts involved early on in the planning phase. Stay safe!
