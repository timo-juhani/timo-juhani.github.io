Foxconn has halted their production in two factories due to Covid outbreaks in China. Foxconn has a key role in component manufacturing for electronics which is the reason why this is expected to add to the problems in supply chains caused by Covid. 
 
Chinese hackers seem to be moving attention from Asia to Russia. Some of the groups have been seen to start deploying malware into Russia’s computing systems and military systems. This surprising turn of events must be investigated more in the coming threat updates. 
 
New facts on Lapsu$ methodologies have trcled in as the police investigation continues. A security company got a hold of their chat messages which confirmed their method of procedure attacking their victims. Lapsu$ criminals used compromised user accounts to recon and steal valuable information. If the credentials were changed, they just purchased another one and continued.
 
We’ve seen targeting of the expired internet domains in the past and this week we must remind ourselves about this. Domain name mimicking combined with phishing is a common vector. However, the specific one for this week are domains that are waiting to expire. Ongoing targeting of expired or soon to expire domains is quite common since it provides an easy way to orchestrate realistic-feeling scams. 
 
Zscaler has published a phishing report recently. In their research they found an increase of 110% in phishing attacks targeted to government agencies (in US but expect same trend in western countries in general). The means of phishing cover the traditional email phishing but more increasingly voice and SMS phishing too. Writer makes a note at this point: For instance, Twitter is seemingly now on a path of transformation and Musk is asking for new features such as end-to-end encryption of direct messages we should expect to see more and more malware and phishing activity there. The benefit of end to end encryption is undebatable but there are some downfalls. One of them is phishing and malware that now traverse through the platform fully encrypted from a source to a destination. That’s why as we gain more confidentiality by means of encryption additional protection layers should be placed around the user such as user white-listing the senders and authentication of all communication parties. 
 
Conti ransomware has taken out a wind turbine company Nordex’s network. Vestas a Danish wind turbine manufacturer was his by another ransomware last November. While the attacks often target internal company networks in Nordex’s case that became problematic to the electricity production. Their turbine remote management system was accessed via the company network. The same old wisdom still applies: categorize systems, segment the systems and enforce the segments.
