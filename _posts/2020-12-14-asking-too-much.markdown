I've been following government responses on cyber and infosec incidents and internet mass surveillance for years now. I even wrote few starry-eyed posts asking for ownership. This year, however, I got educated: Perhaps I'm asking too much.

From the user's perspective only real visibile cue that something is happening on the regulation front are the prompts asking for accepting cookies when you visit web sites. Frankly, that does nothing to bring control over mass surveillance economy that is the defacto business model for most significant web companies.

So, if this is the best tangible result after years and rounds of international legislation, it's no wonder why bigger global economies have developed or are in process of developing their own social media platforms, for instance. Obviously this topic of geo-platforms is more complicated than just that.

The Finnish [Vastaamo](https://yle.fi/uutiset/osasto/news/vastaamo_board_fires_ceo_says_he_kept_data_breach_secret_for_year_and_a_half/11614603) case is an oustanding example. If you rule out the normal internal responses of firing the executive(s) in charge, nothing has happened. The lack of public response from any authority is mindboggling. Simultaneously, individual users are being extorted to regain their data gathered in private therapy sessions.

The [Garmin](https://www.businessinsider.com/garmin-paid-multimillion-dollar-ransom-to-hackers-report-2020-8?r=US&IR=T) case further proved the point that you can be breached and potentially lose very sensitive health and position data without being held accountable. Garmin provides services in Europe and according to their [privacy policy](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/privacy/global/policy/) adhere to GDPR - and most likely many other privacy laws globally.

Therefore they should've notified their users and explained whether any data was lost. At the time of writing, I did many searches and didn't find any public statement of the impact to the end user's privacy. Garmin ended up paying the ransom but it's still unknown how they improved their security after the incident and how they got hacked.

No significant improvements are in horizon for 2021. Sadly, it's the opposite. We should expect new incidents reported with similar narratives outlined in this post.
