I'm relaunching the blog after a period of silence. The old posts will be migrated here over time. The spark came during my long paternity leave. I had time to rethink some of the things I'm doing currently. While posting has proven to be valuable tool for me to digest information so that it's understood and remembered. The process of posting has been problematic. So I had to do changes.

I've been using Twitter, LinekdIn and Worpress (.com) to share my posts. To keep it short, my approach has been without a clear strategy or a plan which is asking for trouble. If you want to work an idea, it should be easy. Much like jotting notes with a pen and a notebook. I don't want to spend time thinking which platform I should post on, how long the payload should be and how to arrange the inter-platform posting. 

I somewhat enjoy Twitter since it's simple and condensed. Fundamentally it is solid. However, for my purpose it seems too condensed. And to be honest I'm not the biggest fan of the atmosphere on that platform. Often a dishonest opinion becomes a mass reality. In addition you will get all the negatives an ad-focused social media comes with. So my approach with Twitter is the same as for protests and demonstrations: interesting to observe from the sidelines but not from where the beer bottles and riot shields clash.

LinkedIn is worse in retaining older posts. It seems like a shoot and forget type of activity which doesn't fit the purpose of this experiment. The whole idea of a log is that it's an organized collection of brief bits of information. 

Wordpress became too much for me. I can't commit to the workflow. It's pure pain and not in a good way. I will be shutting it down. The tools I use must be easily accessible and simple. So while the site might work well for a wedding planner (no offense, you're all great) it's not for me. It's not my goal to spend time on a prose editor and spend hours doing a simple post. The primary objective is to work and the secondary objective is to discuss the work.

This is a GitHub Pages based blog and it seems to answer my needs. The workflow is based on typical Git process which I enjoy. I get my engineering satisfaction from writing the posts on a code editor, pushing the posts over a simple SSH connection and running a local version of the blog on my laptop to preview changes before commiting them. Let alone that it supports well short and long posts. Ok, enough for now.
