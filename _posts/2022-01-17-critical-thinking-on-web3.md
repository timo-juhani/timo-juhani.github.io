Web3 has been promoted as the decentralized version of the Internet. As a DIY enthusiast, Internet purist and a life-long opponent of "do what you're told to do because I said you so" this Web3 hype has been so interesting to me. I put a lot of hope in technologies building on top of Blockchains but I try to exercise quite critical criticism to identify shortfalls of the technology. Trust but verify type of a thing. 

Moxie Marlinspike the former CEO of Signal (recently announced by himself) has written a comprehensive blog post on the current state of Web3 based on his own first impressions after doing a few simple Web3 PoCs and - of course creating Signal: [link to the blog post](https://moxie.org/2022/01/07/web3-first-impressions.html)

The thing I was left pondering was whether I'm suffering from subjective bias. His blog post is aligned to my opinion and previous findings working with the technology. It's easy to like your own ideas unfortunately.
